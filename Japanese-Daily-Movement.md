# Six Daily Movements of Japanese Elders

- https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=8KW4sG3OoME

This file documents the six movements practiced daily by Japanese elders to promote longevity, mobility, and health. These exercises are traditionally integrated into daily routines and can be performed without special equipment.

## 1. Squatting
- **Description:** Repeated squatting and rising movements are common, not only as an exercise but as part of tasks such as gardening, using traditional toilets, or meal preparation on tatami mats.
- **Benefits:** Strengthens legs, hips, and core; preserves mobility.

## 2. Floor Sitting and Rising
- **Description:** Frequent transitions from sitting on the floor (seiza or cross-legged) to standing.
- **Benefits:** Improves lower body strength, flexibility, and balance.

## 3. Stooping and Bending
- **Description:** Elders often stoop and bend while cleaning, gardening, or picking up items, which naturally encourages gentle stretching.
- **Benefits:** Maintains back, hamstring, and hip flexibility.

## 4. Walking
- **Description:** Daily walking to shops, parks, or community centers is a cornerstone of Japanese elder activity.
- **Benefits:** Supports cardiovascular health, stamina, and mental well-being.

## 5. Reaching and Stretching
- **Description:** Reaching for shelves, stretching up during chores, or performing morning calisthenics (radio taiso).
- **Benefits:** Enhances shoulder, arm, and upper body mobility.

## 6. Light Carrying and Lifting
- **Description:** Carrying shopping, lifting household items, or tending to small loads in gardening.
- **Benefits:** Preserves functional strength and bone health.

---

## Notes
- These movements are embedded in daily life rather than performed as separate workouts.
- Practicing these regularly can help maintain joint health, muscle tone, and overall physical independence.
